B2. Terrestrial & Freshwater Ecosystem Changes: Studies forests, soils, rivers, lakes, and related systems under climate impacts.
B3. Marine & Coastal Ecosystem Changes: Examines coral reefs, fisheries, coastal habitats, etc. affected by warming.

**C. Human Systems & Socioeconomic Impacts:**
C1. Agriculture & Food Security: Analyzes crops, forestry, livestock, fruits, food access, and security, etc. under climate stress.
C2. Water Resources & Hydrological Impacts: Evaluates floods, droughts, runoff, groundwater, etc. under changing climates.
C3. Human Health & Well-being: Investigates disease, heat stress, air quality, and related health risks.
C4. Social Equity, Vulnerability & Migration: Considers inequality, vulnerability, displacement, migration, etc. from climate impacts.
C5. Urban Systems & Infrastructure Impacts: Examines risks to cities, transport, energy, buildings, etc. under climate change.
C6. Service & Industry Sector Impacts: Studies services, tourism, industry, markets, etc. influenced by climate change.

**D. Adaptation Strategies:**
D1. Agricultural & Food System Adaptation: Develops crops, forestry, livestock, fruits, and practices for climate resilience, etc.
D2. Urban Planning, Adaptation & Resilience: Builds resilient cities through planning, green infrastructure, adaptation, etc.
D3. Public Health Adaptation: Strengthens health systems, surveillance, services, etc. to protect communities from climate risks.
D4. Public Awareness, Communication & Community Engagement: Promotes environmental awareness, communication, and collective action, countering climate skepticism.
D5. Natural Resource Management & Conservation: Conserves ecosystems, biodiversity, and resources, applying protection and restoration, etc.

**E. Mitigation Mechanisms:**
E1. Climate Policy, Governance & Finance Mechanism: Enacts policies, governance, treaties, carbon pricing, finance, etc. to reduce emissions.
E2. Energy Transition: Advances renewable energy, electrification, efficiency, smart grids, etc. to replace fossil fuels.
E3. Corporate & Industry Climate Action: Drives corporate responsibility, decarbonization, and sustainability initiatives, etc.
E4. Land Use & Ecosystem-based Mitigation: Enhances forests, soils, land-use management, etc. to store carbon.
E5. Transport & Building Emissions Reduction: Cuts emissions through efficient, low-carbon transport, buildings, retrofits, etc.
